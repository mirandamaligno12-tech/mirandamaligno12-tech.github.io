People go to Genova for sightseeing, I go for Pasta!

Ingredients

For the Pesto:

  • 2 cups (packed) fresh Genovese basil leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (lightly toasted)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • 2 tablespoons grated Pecorino (optional, traditional)
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt, to taste

For the Pasta and Vegetables:

  • 400g (14 oz) trofie or trenette pasta (can substitute linguine or spaghetti)
  • 1–2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced into small cubes
  • 150g (about 1 cup) green beans, trimmed and cut into 2–3 inch pieces
  • Salt for pasta water

Preparation Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  2. Add the diced potatoes and green beans to the boiling water.
  3. After 5 minutes, add the pasta to the same pot. Cook all together until the pasta is al dente.
  4. While everything cooks, prepare the pesto:
    • In a mortar and pestle (or food processor), crush garlic and a pinch of salt.
    • Add basil and continue grinding until you get a paste.
    • Add pine nuts and cheeses, grinding or blending into a thick mixture.
    • Slowly drizzle in olive oil while mixing until smooth but slightly textured.
  5. Reserve about 1/4 cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta, potatoes, and beans together.
  6. In a large bowl (or back in the pot, off heat), toss the hot pasta and vegetables with the pesto.
  7. Add a spoonful of pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce and help it coat evenly.
  8. Serve immediately, topped with extra Parmigiano if desired.

Notes from Me

  • Never heat the pesto directly, it loses its flavor and vibrant color.
  • Use young, small basil leaves, no store bought crap
  • Pecorino adds sharpness, use it in moderation along with Parmigiano.(hhaha kidding, use how much you want, no moderation)
