People go to Genova for sightseeing, I go for Pasta!

Ingredients

For the Pesto:

  • 2 cups (packed) fresh Genovese basil leaves
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts (lightly toasted)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • 2 tablespoons grated Pecorino (optional, traditional)
  • 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt, to taste

For the Pasta and Vegetables:

  • 400g (14 oz) trofie or trenette pasta (or linguine/spaghetti)
  • 1–2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 150g (about 1 cup) green beans, trimmed
  • Salt for pasta water

Preparation Steps

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  • Add the diced potatoes and green beans to the pot.
  • After 5 minutes, add the pasta and cook until al dente.
  • While cooking, prepare the pesto:
    • Crush garlic and salt using a mortar or processor.
    • Add basil and grind into a paste.
    • Add pine nuts and cheeses; blend together.
    • Slowly drizzle in olive oil until smooth.
  • Reserve 1/4 cup pasta water, then drain everything.
  • Toss pasta and veggies with pesto off the heat.
  • Add pasta water if needed to loosen the sauce.
  • Serve immediately with extra Parmigiano.

Notes from Me

  • Never heat the pesto — it loses flavor and color!
  • Use small, young basil leaves — not store-bought.
  • Pecorino adds sharpness — mix with Parmigiano.
    • (Haha, just use as much cheese as you like!)
