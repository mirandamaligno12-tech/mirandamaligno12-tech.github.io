Beautifully cooked meat, a taste of PARMA

Ingredients

For the Ragu

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 150g ground beef (high quality, not too lean)
  • 150g ground pork or veal
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 400g (14 oz) canned peeled tomatoes, crushed
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

For the Pasta:

  • 400g (14 oz) fresh tagliatelle or fettuccine
  • Salt for the pasta water

To Serve:

  • Freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano (from Parma, of course)

Preparation Steps

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onion, carrot, and celery (soffritto). Sauté gently until soft and fragrant (about 8–10 minutes).
  3. Add the ground pork and beef. Break up the meat and cook until browned.
  4. Pour in the white wine. Let it simmer until it evaporates completely.
  5. Add the crushed tomatoes and stir well. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Lower the heat to a gentle simmer. Cook uncovered for at least 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
  7. If the sauce dries out too much, add a bit of water or broth to keep it moist.
  8. Boil salted water in a large pot and cook the pasta until al dente.
  9. Drain the pasta and toss with the ragù in a warm pan or serving bowl.
  10. add the pasta water!
  11. Serve immediately with a generous amount of Parmigiano-Reggiano grated on top.

Notes & Tips

  • No garlic or herbs true Bolognese is all about meat,and beauty.
  • Use fresh tagliatelle, not spaghetti. This is tradition in Bologna and Parma.
  • Slow cooking is the key for beauty low and slow for deep flavor.
