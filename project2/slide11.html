A beautiful Easy, simple dish made with love!!

Ingredients

Classic Italian Version:

  • 400g (14 oz) fettuccine
  • 100g (7 tbsp) unsalted butter
  • 100g (1 cup) freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
  • Salt, to taste
  • Optional: a few tablespoons of pasta water to help emulsify

American Creamy Version (optional):

  • 400g (14 oz) fettuccine
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Optional: garlic or nutmeg (a small pinch)

Preparation Steps

For the Classic Version:

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the fettuccine until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, melt the butter in a large warm bowl (not on direct heat).
  3. Drain the pasta, reserving a little pasta water.
  4. Add the hot pasta directly into the bowl with melted butter. Toss to coat well.
  5. Sprinkle in the grated Parmigiano a little at a time, tossing continuously to create a creamy sauce.
  6. If needed, add a tablespoon or two of pasta water to help emulsify the cheese and butter.
  7. Serve immediately, with extra cheese on top if desired.

For the American Creamy Version:

  1. Cook the fettuccine in salted boiling water until al dente.
  2. In a saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Add cream and stir gently.
  3. Simmer for 2–3 minutes, then stir in the Parmesan cheese until melted and smooth.
  4. Season with salt and pepper. Optional: add a pinch of nutmeg or garlic for flavor.
  5. Drain the pasta and toss it with the sauce in the pan.
  6. Serve hot with extra grated cheese on top.

Notes from me

  • Use high-quality cheese real Parmigiano-Reggiano makes a huge difference.
  • Don’t overheat the formaggio or it may clump, let the heat of the pasta do the work.
  • The original Alfredo in Rome is just butter and cheese AND no cream!
  • Serve immediately — Alfredo doesn’t reheat well.
