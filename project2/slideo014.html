Beautiful, Simple, bold, and truly Roman.

Ingredients

For the Pasta:

• 400g (14 oz) dried pasta (typically bucatini or rigatoni)

For the Sauce:

• 150g (5 oz) guanciale (cured pork jowl), sliced into thin strips
• 400g (14 oz) canned peeled tomatoes (San Marzano recommended)
• 50g (1/2 cup) grated Pecorino Romano cheese
• 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil (optional)
• 1 small dried chili pepper (peperoncino), crumbled (optional)
• Salt, to taste

Preparation Steps

• Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil for the pasta.
• In a wide skillet, cook the guanciale (and a little olive oil if needed) over low to medium heat.
• Let the guanciale slowly render its fat and become golden and crispy (about 5–7 minutes).
• If using, add the crumbled dried chili pepper and stir briefly.
• Remove the guanciale with a slotted spoon and set aside. Leave the rendered fat in the pan.
• Crush the canned tomatoes by hand or with a spoon, then add them to the pan with the guanciale fat.
• Simmer the sauce gently for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened.
• Meanwhile, cook the pasta until al dente according to package instructions.
• Drain the pasta, reserving a small cup of pasta water.
• Add the drained pasta to the sauce and toss to coat. Add a bit of reserved pasta water if needed.
• Return the crispy guanciale to the pan and mix everything well.
• Remove from heat and sprinkle with grated Pecorino Romano. Toss again to combine.
• Serve immediately, topped with extra Pecorino if desired.

Notes from Me!

• Use guanciale, not pancetta — it's the authentic ingredient.
• No garlic or onion — traditional Amatriciana is minimal and focused.
• Salt carefully — both Pecorino and guanciale are already salty.
• Recommended pasta: Bucatini is classic, but spaghetti or rigatoni also work well.
