Pasta with Sugo, can't get any better!!!

Ingredients

For the Sauce:

  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2–3 garlic cloves, minced or smashed
  • 1 can (28 oz / 800g) whole peeled San Marzano tomatoes
  • Salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Pinch of sugar (optional)
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves

For the Pasta:

  • 400–500g (about 1 lb) dried pasta (spaghetti, rigatoni, or penne)
  • Salt for the pasta water

    • Ingredients (4 servings)

    Preparation Steps

    1. Heat olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat.
    2. Add chopped onion and sauté until soft and translucent (about 5–7 minutes).
    3. Add minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds until fragrant — do not brown.
    4. Pour in the can of whole peeled tomatoes. Crush them with a spoon or by hand.
    5. Season with salt and pepper. Add a pinch of sugar if tomatoes are too acidic.
    6. Let the sauce simmer gently, uncovered, for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    7. About 5 minutes before the sauce is done, tear in the fresh basil leaves.
    8. Optional: stir in a knob of butter for extra richness.
    9. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
    10. Cook the pasta until al dente according to package instructions.
    11. Reserve ½ cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.
    12. Add the drained pasta to the sauce and toss to coat well.
    13. If needed, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to loosen the sauce.
    14. Cook together for 1–2 minutes so the pasta absorbs the flavors.
    15. Serve immediately, topped with fresh basil and (optional) grated cheese.

    Tips from Me/Nonna

    • Don't overcook the Pasta
    • Salt your pasta water generously.
    • ADD THE PASTA WATER
    • DO not BREAK THE SPAGHETTI!
    • Let the pasta and sauce hug each other — don’t drown it.
    • Do Not put KETCHUP ON PASTA!
